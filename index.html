Investing Basics

Learn what options are available and simulate how your money grows

Fixed Deposit (FD)

Low risk

Bank deposit earning guaranteed interest over a fixed term.

Return: 6–7.5% p.a. · DICGC insured up to ₹5L
Best for: conservative savers, short-term goals. Penalty applies for early withdrawal. Interest is taxable as income. Best banks currently offer 7–7.5% p.a.

Mutual Funds

Medium risk

Pool of money from many investors, managed by professionals.

Return: 10–14% p.a. · SEBI regulated · SIP from ₹500/mo
Best for: long-term wealth creation. SIP automates monthly investing. Index funds have lower expense ratios (0.1–0.5%).

ETF / Index Funds

Medium risk

Tracks a market index (like Nifty 50) and trades like a stock.

Return: 11–13% p.a. · Very low fees · High liquidity
Best for: passive investors who want market returns. Lower cost than active funds. Requires a demat account to trade.

PPF / ELSS

Low–medium risk

Tax-saving options with government backing or equity exposure.

PPF: 7.1% guaranteed · ELSS: market-linked, 3yr lock-in
PPF has a 15-year lock-in but is completely tax-free. ELSS qualifies for ₹1.5L deduction under Section 80C. Both excellent for tax planning.
